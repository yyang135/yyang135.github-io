Analyze of World of Warcraft Battleground by Yiming Yang¶

0. Introduction¶

When I was in high school, I have played so much World of Warcraft. I love RPG game, and World of Warcraft is one of my favorite games. World of Warcraft is a massively multiplayer online role-playing game (MMORPG) released in 2004 by Blizzard Entertainment. After it came out, it became one of the most popular games. World of Warcraft was the world's most popular MMORPG by player count of nearly 10 million in 2009. It is the icon of MMORPG.

At the beginning, players will create their own character. They need to determine the class, which includes Death Knight, Demon Hunter, Druid, Hunter, Mage, Monk, Paladin, Priest, Rogue, Shaman, Warlock, and Warrior. Also, players need to determine their faction. There are two fractions, Horde and Alliance, and they are opposite.

Battlegrounds are instanced areas used for player versus player combat (PvP). In them players can participate in team PvP, struggling for victory in one of a number of battlegrounds against a similarly capable team of players from the opposite faction.

If you still want more information: Official website of WOW: https://worldofwarcraft.com/en-us/ This has everything you want to know about WOW(classes, faction, game mode): https://wow.gamepedia.com/Wowpedia More information about Battleground: https://wow.gamepedia.com/Battleground

In this tutorial, I will do an analyze on the winrate Battleground of World of Warcraft, which effect or determine the winrate. I will try to analyze if player's number of kills and deaths, damage and heal will effect the winrate, the winrate related to classes, faction, and maps. I will see if there is relationship between those.

And then we will read the data, I find the data from Kaggle, which is the record and data of some random matches. In this dataset the meaning of columns are the following:

  1. Battleground: The name of battleground or the map (AB: Arathi basin. - BG: Battle for Gilneas. - DG: Deepwind gorge. - ES: Eye of the storm. - SA: Strand of the ancients. - SM: Silvershard mines. - SS: Seething shore. - TK: Temple of Kotmogu. - TP: Twin peaks. - WG: Warsong gulch.)
  2. Code: code for the battleground.
  3. Faction: faction of the player (Horde or Alliance).
  4. Class: class of the player (warrior, paladin, hunter, rogue, priest, death knight, shaman, mage, warlock, monk, druid, demon hunter).
  5. KB: number of mortal kills given by the player.
  6. D: number of times that the player died.
  7. HK: number of killings where the player or his/her group contributed.
  8. DD: damage done by the player.
  9. HD: healing done by the player.
  10. Honor: honor awarded to the player.
  11. Win: 1 if the player won.
  12. Lose: 1 if the player lost.
  13. Rol: dps if the player is a damage dealer; heal if the player is focused in healing allies. Not all classes can be healers, just shaman, paladin, priest, monk and druid, but all classes can be damage dealers.
  14. BE: some weeks there is a bonus event, when the honor gained is increased. 1 if the battleground happened during that week.

For, our EDA analysis, it looks like KD ratio, KR, damage and heal from dps is not related to winrate. Heal's damage and heal is probably related to winrate, and heal wins more than dps, which means heal may be more likely to win battleground. Moreover, Horde is more likely to win.
